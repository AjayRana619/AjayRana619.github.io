Why Tokyo in 2026

The World's Most
Layered City

Tokyo in 2026 remains one of the most extraordinary destinations on Earth — ancient temples beside skyscraper forests, the world's best ramen for $8, and a subway network so reliable you could set your watch to it.

This guide is built around a realistic mid-range budget: comfortable business hotels, proper sit-down meals, and no skimping on the experiences that make Tokyo unforgettable. All prices are in USD with yen equivalents, using the approximate 2026 rate of ¥148–152 per $1 USD.

01

Shibuya & Harajuku

Tokyo's beating, neon-drenched heart — the crossing, the fashion, the chaos
Est. $80–$100
Morning · 9:00 AM
Shibuya Crossing
The world's busiest scramble crossing — best viewed from the Mag's Park rooftop or Starbucks Reserve Roastery. Watch the morning rush from above before descending into the flow.
FREE (viewing) · ¥700 Starbucks coffee
Mid-Morning · 10:30 AM
Meiji Shrine
A serene forested sanctuary 10 minutes from Harajuku Station. The contrast with Shibuya's energy is startling. Arrive early for a quiet morning among the towering torii.
FREE entry
Noon · 12:30 PM
Takeshita Street, Harajuku
Crepe shops, vintage fashion boutiques, and the full spectrum of Tokyo street style. Budget ¥600–¥800 for a legendary Harajuku crepe while people-watching.
Crepe: ~$5 · Shopping: variable
Afternoon · 3:00 PM
Shibuya Sky Observatory
The rooftop observation deck on Scramble Square offers the most dramatic 360° panorama in Tokyo, including a clear view down into the crossing. Book online to skip lines and save ¥200.
¥2,000 (~$13) · Book in advance
Evening · 7:00 PM
Dinner in Dogenzaka
Shibuya's backstreet restaurant alleys offer everything from yakitori under the train tracks to ramen counters and affordable izakayas. Budget ¥1,500–¥2,500 for a full meal with a beer.
¥1,500–¥2,500 (~$10–$17)
Night · 9:00 PM
Konbini Night Cap
End the evening the Tokyo way: a 7-Eleven or Lawson convenience store run. Japanese convenience store food — onigiri, sandwiches, hot oden — is genuinely excellent. Stock up for tomorrow's breakfast too.
¥500–¥900 (~$3–$6)

02

Shinjuku

Skyscrapers by day, neon-soaked alleys by night — the city in full contrast
Est. $85–$110
Morning · 9:30 AM
Shinjuku Gyoen Garden
One of Japan's finest national gardens, covering 58 hectares with Japanese, French, and English garden styles. A peaceful morning retreat before the city's energy picks up.
¥500 (~$3)
Late Morning · 11:30 AM
Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building
Free observation deck on the 45th floor — arguably the best free panoramic view in Tokyo. On clear days you can see as far as Mt. Fuji. Come back at sunset for the full experience.
FREE · Open until 10:30 PM
Afternoon · 1:00 PM
Omoide Yokocho (Memory Lane)
Narrow alleys beneath the Shinkansen tracks lined with tiny yakitori stalls. Smoke, sizzle, and salarymen. Lunch here is one of Tokyo's great sensory experiences — even better at night.
Lunch: ¥1,200–¥2,000 (~$8–$14)
Afternoon · 3:00 PM
Shinjuku Golden Gai
A grid of over 200 tiny bars, each seating 5–8 people, each with its own personality. Browse the alleyways in the afternoon before the crowds hit. Pick your bar for the evening now.
Bar drinks: ¥600–¥1,200/each (~$4–$8)
Evening · 6:30 PM
Kabukicho & Don Quijote
Shinjuku's entertainment district comes alive at night. Browse the surreal Don Quijote discount chain for unique souvenirs, then grab ramen or a kaisendon rice bowl at one of the many nearby restaurants.
Dinner: ¥1,500–¥2,500 (~$10–$17)
Night · 9:00 PM
Golden Gai Nightcap
Return to that bar you scouted earlier. Most have a seating cover charge (¥500–¥1,000) but the experience of squeezing into a 6-seat bar with a local bartender is priceless.
¥1,500–¥2,500 (~$10–$17) total

03

Asakusa & Old Tokyo

Senso-ji, rickshaws, river walks, and the taste of Edo-era Japan
Est. $70–$90
Early Morning · 7:30 AM
Senso-ji Temple at Dawn
Tokyo's oldest and most famous temple is transformative at dawn before the crowds. Walk through the Kaminarimon Gate, along Nakamise shopping street (still shuttered), and into the main hall for morning prayers.
FREE entry to temple grounds
Morning · 9:00 AM
Nakamise Shopping Street
When the shops open, browse Japan's oldest shopping street for traditional snacks (ningyo-yaki cakes, ningyoyaki, kaminari-okoshi rice crackers) and handmade crafts. Great for authentic souvenirs.
Snacks: ¥300–¥800 (~$2–$5)
Mid-Morning · 10:30 AM
Sumida River Walk & Skytree
Walk along the Sumida River to Tokyo Skytree — the world's tallest tower at 634m. The lower Tembo Deck at 350m offers breathtaking views. Book tickets online to save time and a small discount.
Tembo Deck: ¥2,100 (~$14) · online booking recommended
Afternoon · 1:00 PM
Lunch: Asakusa Soba
Asakusa is the heartland of traditional Tokyo soba. Find a tiny counter restaurant serving hand-cut buckwheat noodles in dashi broth. This is unpretentious, perfect Japanese lunch food.
¥1,000–¥1,500 (~$7–$10)
Afternoon · 2:30 PM
Ueno Park & Museums
Take the subway to Ueno, home to Tokyo's greatest cluster of world-class museums. The Tokyo National Museum holds Japan's largest collection of national treasures. The park itself is free and beautiful.
Tokyo National Museum: ¥1,000 (~$7) · Park: FREE
Evening · 7:00 PM
Dinner: Tempura in Asakusa
Asakusa's Kaminarimon area has excellent traditional tempura restaurants at lunch and dinner prices. Try a tendon (tempura on rice bowl) — deeply satisfying, deeply Tokyo.
¥1,500–¥2,800 (~$10–$19)

04

Mt. Fuji Day Trip

Japan's sacred summit — Fuji Five Lakes, the 5th Station, and mountain magic
Est. $85–$120
Very Early · 6:00 AM
Shinjuku → Kawaguchiko (Bus)
The Keio Highway Bus from Shinjuku Bus Terminal to Kawaguchiko Lake takes about 2 hours. This is the most scenic and affordable way to reach the Fuji Five Lakes area. Book tickets a few days ahead through the Keio Bus website.
¥2,200 one-way (~$15) · 2 hrs
Morning · 8:30 AM
Lake Kawaguchiko
On a clear morning, the reflection of Mt. Fuji in the lake is simply one of the most spectacular sights in Japan. Rent a bicycle (¥500/hour) and ride the scenic lakeside path for the classic framed-Fuji photo.
Bicycle rental: ¥500/hr (~$3)
Midday · 11:00 AM
Fuji 5th Station (Fujinomiya)
A local bus or taxi from Kawaguchiko reaches the 5th Station at 2,300m — the highest point you can reach without climbing. In summer, you can begin the hiking trail upward. The views above the cloud line are otherworldly.
Bus from Kawaguchi: ¥1,500 (~$10) · Trail: FREE
Afternoon · 1:30 PM
Lunch at the 5th Station
The 5th Station has several restaurants and food stalls. Try hot Fuji-brand curry or udon — eating warm food at 2,300m surrounded by clouds and mountain air is a memory you'll keep forever.
¥1,200–¥1,800 (~$8–$12)
Late Afternoon · 3:30 PM
Oshino Hakkai (8 Springs)
On the way back, stop at this UNESCO-listed village of eight crystal-clear springs fed by Fuji's snowmelt. The water is impossibly clear and the thatched farmhouses give you feudal Japan in 15 minutes.
~¥250 (~$2) area entry
Evening · 6:30 PM
Return to Tokyo (Bus)
Evening buses from Kawaguchiko back to Shinjuku run frequently. Book your return in advance, especially on weekends, as these sell out. Arrive back at Shinjuku around 8:30 PM — dinner in the city to celebrate.
¥2,200 return (~$15) · Hotel dinner: ~$15

05

Tokyo DisneySea

The most beautifully designed theme park on Earth — a full-day commitment
Est. $130–$160
Early · 7:30 AM
Train to Maihama Station
Take the JR Keiyo Line from Tokyo Station to Maihama — about 15 minutes and under $2 each way on Suica. Then hop the Disney Resort Line monorail to the park entrance. Be at the gates when they open at 9 AM sharp — the first 30 minutes are magical with no crowds.
JR train: ¥290 (~$2) · Monorail: ¥260 (~$2)
Park Opens · 9:00 AM
Journey to Center of the Earth (Priority)
This is DisneySea's signature ride — a Jules Verne-inspired plunge through an active volcano. Get there first thing or use the Disney Premier Access ($15–$20 extra) to skip lines later in the day.
Included in admission · Premier Access: ¥2,000–¥2,500
Mid-Morning · 11:00 AM
Mediterranean Harbor & Shows
The heart of DisneySea is the most beautiful theme park "land" ever designed — a perfect replica of a Mediterranean port. Multiple water shows and character parades run from this harbor. Check the schedule at the gate.
Included in admission
Lunch · 12:30 PM
Dining in the Park
DisneySea has genuinely excellent food compared to most theme parks — try the gyoza dog, the Mickey-shaped bao, or a full sit-down at the Magellan's restaurant. Budget accordingly: full meals are theme-park priced but the quality justifies it.
Quick service: ¥1,500 · Sit-down: ¥4,000–¥8,000
Afternoon · 3:00 PM
Indiana Jones, 20,000 Leagues & More
DisneySea packs 7 distinct themed "ports of call." Don't miss 20,000 Leagues Under the Sea, Tower of Terror (themed entirely differently from American parks), and the Toy Story Mania games area for a break from lines.
Included in admission
Evening · 7:00 PM
Fantasmic! Night Show
DisneySea's nighttime spectacular on the Mediterranean Harbor is a breathtaking projection, fireworks, and water show. Stake out a spot 45 minutes early at the harbor's edge. An unforgettable final evening in Tokyo.
Included in admission

Insider Tips
& Practical
Advice

🚇

Get a Suica Card Immediately

Load ¥5,000 on a Suica IC card at Narita or Haneda airport. Tap in and out of every subway, JR train, and bus — and pay at convenience stores, vending machines, and most restaurants. Crucial for Tokyo.

🏨

Best Areas to Stay (Mid-Range)

Shinjuku and Shibuya put you central to everything. Asakusa is cheaper and charming. Budget ¥18,000–¥22,000/night ($120–$150) for a clean, good-location business hotel. Book Toyoko Inn, APA Hotel, or Dormy Inn.

🍜

Eat Like a Local, Spend Like a Budget Traveler

Lunch set menus at restaurants (11am–2pm) often include full meals for ¥800–¥1,200 ($5–$8). Convenience stores (7-Eleven, FamilyMart) have excellent hot food. Save splurge meals for ramen, sushi counters, or tempura specialists.

📱

Get a Tourist SIM or eSIM

Buy a Japan data SIM at the airport (¥3,000–¥5,000 for 2 weeks). Major carriers: IIJmio, Sakura Mobile, AiRSIM eSIM. You need data for Google Maps navigation — Tokyo's subway system is complex enough without it.

🎟️

Book DisneySea Well in Advance

Tickets must be purchased online at tokyodisneyresort.jp — they no longer sell at the gate. Adult weekday tickets run ¥7,900–¥9,400 (~$52–$63); weekend prices climb to ¥10,900 (~$73). Select your date and buy 1–2 months ahead.

💴

Cash Is Still King

Many smaller restaurants, temples, and traditional shops are cash-only. Use 7-Eleven ATMs — they reliably accept international Visa/Mastercard. Withdraw ¥30,000–¥50,000 at the start and replenish as needed.

🗻

Fuji Visibility Is Never Guaranteed

Mt. Fuji is famously shy — clouds obscure the summit on 60–70% of days in summer. Go early (weekday mornings in autumn or winter are best). Check the live Fuji webcam the evening before your planned day trip.

🆓

The Best Things Are Free

Meiji Shrine, Senso-ji grounds, Shinjuku Gyoen (¥500), Tokyo Metropolitan Building observatory, Yoyogi Park, Hamarikyu Gardens, Ginza galleries, and most neighborhood walks cost nothing but time.

Where to Publish
Your Travel Blog

The best free and low-cost platforms to turn this Tokyo trip into a travel website

WordPress.com
Best for serious bloggers

Powers 40% of the internet. The free plan gives you a subdomain (yourblog.wordpress.com), unlimited posts, and built-in SEO. Upgrade to a paid plan (~$4/month) for a custom domain and monetization.

  • Massive plugin ecosystem
  • Best long-term SEO potential
  • Thousands of travel templates
  • Easiest to monetize as you grow
Free plan available
Wix
Best drag-and-drop builder

100+ travel-specific templates, intuitive visual editor, and built-in SEO tools. The free plan shows Wix ads; remove them from $16/month. Great if you want beautiful design without coding.

  • 100+ travel blog templates
  • No coding required
  • Built-in SEO & analytics
  • Social media integration
Free plan available
Travellerspoint
Best travel-specific platform

A dedicated travel blogging community with interactive trip maps, unlimited photo uploads, and a built-in audience of fellow travelers. Perfect for documenting journeys with geo-tagged entries.

  • Interactive travel maps
  • Built-in travel community
  • Unlimited photo uploads
  • Google Analytics integration
Completely free
Blogger
Google's free platform

Google's own blogging platform has been around since 1999. It's simple, free forever, has no storage limits, and integrates perfectly with Google AdSense — ideal if you want to eventually monetize with ads.

  • Free forever, no limits
  • Easy AdSense monetization
  • Custom domain support
  • Google account integration
Completely free
Medium
Best for audience discovery

Write beautiful, minimal travel stories and tap into Medium's existing readership of millions. Stories can rank in Google quickly. Limited customization, but the writing experience is the cleanest of any platform.

  • Built-in audience of millions
  • Beautiful reading experience
  • Partner Program income potential
  • Fast Google indexing
Free to publish
Substack
Best newsletter + blog combo

Write travel posts that double as an email newsletter. Build a subscriber list from day one. Substack is free to use and takes a 10% cut only when you launch paid subscriptions — perfect for growing an audience.

  • Email list built-in from day one
  • Free until you charge subscribers
  • Great for narrative travel writing
  • Podcast hosting included
Free to start

Pro tip: Start on WordPress.com or Wix for the best long-term SEO. Use Medium or Substack in parallel to distribute your best posts to a wider audience. Travellerspoint is uniquely built for travel storytelling and worth having for the interactive maps alone.
